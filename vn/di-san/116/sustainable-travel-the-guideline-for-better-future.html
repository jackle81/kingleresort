Experience sustainable travel at Avana Retreat

The true essence of tourism lies in how it enhances local authenticity and beauty. Wonderful as the spirit is, the industry will not be able to go in the long run if no responsible commitments and actions are being done. Therefore, sustainable travel is born, with guidelines and goals that offer a promising solution for the future of the tourism industry. 

What is sustainable travel?

Sustainable travel is a type of traversing and discovering a place while honoring the authenticity of its culture, ecology, and people. While developing opportunities for the future, this form of tourism fulfills the demands of current visitors and host communities. With this approach, the three pillars of sustainable tourism: People - Planet - Profit, are all balanced and benefit each other in the long term.

Sustainable travel is easy 

Grand and far-fetched as it may sound, everyone can practice sustainable travel from the little things: Travel by bike instead of cabs, support locals’ products, and choose hotels or travel agents that promote sustainability; these are just some of many actions that you can do to show your commitment to the idea. Whether big or small, your choice will make an impact, on yourself, the region, and society.     

7 things Avana Retreat has done to encourage sustainable travel 

1. Green the empty hills

At Avana and surrounding areas, more than 10 hectares have been restored. In Mai Chau, we are collaborating with the local authorities to reforest other barren slopes. 

2. Use organic products 

Products in the spa and bathroom are 100% made of organic ingredients from local suppliers, giving guests an intimate connection to natural elements 

3. On-site water purifying system 

We build a water purifying system to filter the cleanest water for our guests. Glass bottles are used for complimentary water for the reusable purpose

4. Create jobs for the locals

90% of Avana Retreat’s staff are locals. We provide vocational training and offer job opportunities with great compensation and welfare.

5. Cultural exhibition

Our exhibition area features a range of books, musical instruments, agricultural implements, and other artifacts that highlight minority people's lifestyles.

6. Exciting activities 

Avana Retreat provides trekking trips that explore natural regions with a close connection to ethnic communities. There are also many activities that promote sustainable travel, avoiding giving off extra pollution.

7. Local food source

Fresh ingredients, which come from local suppliers, are sourced and prepped every day by our chefs.

We are proud of our efforts in committing to sustainable travel and are willing to take action for a better future, have a thorough look at our activities here.
